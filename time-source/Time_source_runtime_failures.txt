Solution to this: add Guava 19.0 to POM

  <dependency>
    <groupId>com.google.guava</groupId>
    <artifactId>guava</artifactId>
    <version>19.0</version>
  </dependency>

2. Reactor: missing a class
Caused by: java.lang.ClassNotFoundException: reactor.core.publisher.WorkQueueProcessor
	at java.net.URLClassLoader.findClass(URLClassLoader.java:381) ~[na:1.8.0_65]

Here's the Reactor version:
  io.projectreactor:reactor-core:jar:2.0.8.RELEASE:compile (version managed from 3.0.3.BUILD-SNAPSHOT)

Reference on that "version managed from ..." stuff:
http://stackoverflow.com/questions/26316502/how-to-debug-artifact-replacement-in-maven/26317934#26317934
(basically, "just add that dependency explicitly in your POM")

Note that, in the PubSub binder, I had to upgrade to reactor-core 3.0.3.BUILD-SNAPSHOT.
The reason for that was this error:
Caused by: java.lang.NoSuchMethodError: reactor.core.publisher.Mono.checkpoint()Lreactor/core/publisher/Mono;

So, for consistency WRT Reactor versions, I will add that explicitly here, too:

  <dependency>
    <groupId>io.projectreactor</groupId>
    <artifactId>reactor-core</artifactId>
    <version>3.0.3.BUILD-SNAPSHOT</version>
  </dependency>

That solved my issue, and the time source builds and tests OK.
